The Protocol Code

The Seven Rules

01
PHYSICAL PROTOCOL

1 Hour Workout. Daily 6 Days a Week.

Consistency is key. Devote exactly 60 minutes to high-performance physical execution each day. No cutting sessions short, no excuses. Make fitness non-negotiable.

02
RECOVERY PROTOCOL

1 Rest Day. Weekly.

Dedicate one full day per week to absolute physical recovery. Do not engage in structured workouts. Recharge the nervous system to avoid burnout and emerge stronger.

03
BIOLOGICAL PROTOCOL

3 Litres Water. Daily Quota.

Hydrate your body, keep cognitive function sharp, and flush out metabolic toxins. Log your volume daily.

04
TOXIN REMOVAL

No Cigarettes & Alcohol. Zero Tolerance.

Remove neurological suppressants and carcinogens entirely. Protect your cellular longevity, and maintain absolute mental clarity and alertness.

05
COGNITIVE EXPANSION

10 Mins of Mental Training. Daily.

Train your mind for neuroplasticity. Devote 10 minutes daily to active mental exercises: language learning or practicing a musical instrument. Push cognitive boundaries.

06
LOGGING & INTEGRITY

Daily Checklist & Log. Unbroken.

Log your activities, track your status, and keep record of your path. A protocol not logged is a protocol not managed. Hold yourself 100% accountable using our downloadable tracker PDF below.

Common Questions

What counts as a workout?
Any dedicated physical effort: gym weightlifting, running, outdoor swimming, calisthenics, or yoga. Passive stretching does not count. The session must be a full 60 minutes.
Are the workouts outdoor?
Unlike other programs, the Sigma Challenge workouts can be performed indoors or outdoors, but must run for exactly 60 minutes, 6 days a week, with one full recovery day.
Can I skip logging a day?
No. Unbroken accountability is the core. If you skip logging, you have broken Rule 6 (Daily Checklist & Log), which requires a restart from Day 1.
What counts as mental training?
Activities that actively train your mind for neuroplasticity, such as practicing a musical instrument or active language learning. Passive listening to podcasts or reading for entertainment does not count.
